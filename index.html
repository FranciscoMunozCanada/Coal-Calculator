Step 1 — Initial Moisture Determination (sub-sample)
W₁ — Wet sample
Always start with exactly
50 g of coal from the
mixed batch. Place it in
the Moisture Teller.

This number never
changes.
⊘ standard — do not change
W₂ — Dry sample
After drying at 115°C
for 15 minutes in the
Moisture Teller, weigh
the same sample again.

Type that weight
here (in grams).
✎ enter weight after drying (g)
MC₁ = [ ( W₁ − W₂ ) / W₁ ] × 100
// calculates the current moisture % of your coal sample
Step 2 — Full Batch & Target Moisture
CB — Batch weight
Total weight of the full
coal batch you are
preparing today.

Typical range:
35 to 80 lb.

Type the actual
weight of your batch.
✎ enter batch weight (lb) — 35 to 80 lb
MT — Target moisture
Required for a valid SHO
test: between 3.8%
and 4.2%. Fixed at
4.0% per SOP.

Do not change this.
⊘ standard — do not change
Water (lb) = CB × [ ( MT − MC₁ ) / 100 ] × 1.10
// factor 1.10 offsets moisture lost during mixing
💧
Why × 1.10? The 1.10 factor adds 10% extra water to compensate for moisture lost to evaporation during the 5–10 minute mixing process. This has been validated internally over multiple years of SHO testing by F. Muñoz.
